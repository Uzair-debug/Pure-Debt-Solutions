3. Personal Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

3.1 Information You Provide

  • • Contact Information: Name, email address, phone number, postal address
  • • Financial Information: Details about your debts, creditors, income, expenses, and financial situation
  • • Identity Information: Identity number, proof of identity documents (when required)
  • • Communication: Messages, inquiries, and correspondence you send to us

3.2 Information Automatically Collected

  • • Website Usage: IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, time spent on pages
  • • Cookies and Tracking: We may use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience

4. How We Collect Personal Information

We collect personal information through:

  • • Directly from you when you complete our contact forms, request assessments, or communicate with us
  • • Through our website when you visit, browse, or interact with our services
  • • Via phone calls, WhatsApp messages, or email communications
  • • From third parties (with your consent) such as credit bureaus or creditors when necessary for debt removal services

5. Purpose of Processing Personal Information

We process your personal information for the following lawful purposes:

  • • Service Delivery: To provide debt removal assessment and services, communicate with you about your case, and process your requests
  • • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal obligations
  • • Communication: To respond to your inquiries, provide customer support, and send important updates
  • • Business Operations: To improve our services, conduct analytics, and manage our business operations
  • • Security: To protect against fraud, unauthorized access, and ensure the security of our systems

6. Legal Basis for Processing

Under POPIA, we process your personal information based on the following legal grounds:

  • • Consent: You have given explicit consent for specific processing activities
  • • Contract Performance: Processing is necessary to perform a contract or take steps before entering into a contract
  • • Legal Obligation: Processing is required to comply with legal obligations
  • • Legitimate Interests: Processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests, provided these do not override your rights

7. Sharing and Disclosure of Personal Information

We may share your personal information only in the following circumstances:

  • • Service Providers: With trusted third-party service providers who assist us in operating our business (e.g., IT services, email services), subject to confidentiality agreements
  • • Creditors and Debt Collectors: When necessary for debt removal services, we may communicate with creditors or debt collection agencies on your behalf
  • • Legal Requirements: When required by law, court order, or government regulation, or to protect our rights and the rights of others
  • • With Your Consent: We may share information with other parties when you have given explicit consent

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

8. Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction:

  • • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • • Secure servers and databases with restricted access
  • • Regular security assessments and updates
  • • Staff training on data protection and confidentiality
  • • Access controls and authentication measures

While we strive to protect your personal information, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law:

  • • Active Clients: Information is retained for the duration of our service relationship and as required for ongoing services
  • • Legal Requirements: We may retain certain information as required by law, regulation, or for legal proceedings
  • • Inactive Records: After services are completed, we retain records for a reasonable period as required by law and business needs, then securely dispose of them

10. Your Rights Under POPIA

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

Right of Access

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, including information about the source of the data and how it is being used.

Right to Correction

You may request correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated personal information.

Right to Deletion

You may request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal and contractual obligations that may require us to retain certain information.

Right to Object

You may object to the processing of your personal information for certain purposes, including direct marketing.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on consent, you have the right to withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before withdrawal.

Right to Complain

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator if you believe your rights under POPIA have been violated.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in Section 12 (Contact Information).
